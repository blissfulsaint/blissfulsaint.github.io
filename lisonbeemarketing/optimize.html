Optimize Your Upsell Price for the Highest Conversion Rate that Maximizes Your Backend Profit

Implementation:

The typical upsell price is 2-3x the frontend offer price, but is that always the best case? Not necessarily.

The core metric to be hyper-focused on is the CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) and AOV (Average Order Value) “spread”.

AKA the difference between these two numbers.

You should always be trying to maximize your AOV on Day 1 and lower your CPA over time to gradually increase the gap between AOV and CPA to maximize your liquidation. When done right, you can turn a profit on day one.

There are two primary ways to test how your upsell pricing and flow will change the AOV of your funnel:

  • Simply do a 50-50 split of the one-time-offer upsell price (i.e. If it’s currently $297, test $197 or $497)
  • Put a lower-priced upsell in front of your higher-priced upsell and make it a no-brainer offer. Price this new upsell about 80% lower than your front-end offer.

Notes:

By placing a lower-priced product in Upsell 1 and making it a no-brainer, highly-congruent, irresistible offer, you’ll get a very high take-rate of that offer. In most cases, as it’s lower than what they’ve already invested, it becomes too hard to say no.

This also enforces another subconscious “Yes” in their head, and therefore may also increase your conversion rate on your higher-priced upsell offer that follows.
